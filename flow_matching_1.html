The Goal — What generative modelling is actually doing
The fundamental setup

You have a dataset of images. You want a model that can generate new images from the same distribution — but you can't directly sample from that distribution. You only have examples of it.

💡 The universal solution: learn a transformation from a simple distribution you can sample (e.g. standard Gaussian noise) into the complex data distribution you want.
The transformation we want to learn
Two approaches
Both learn this P₀ → P₁ transformation, but they go about it very differently.
DIFFUSION
Fix a specific noising recipe, then learn to reverse it step by step.
FLOW MATCHING
Don't fix any process. Just learn a smooth vector field that morphs P₀ into P₁ directly.
Diffusion Models — the Explicit Noising Approach
How diffusion works EXPLICIT PROCESS

Take a clean image. Add a little Gaussian noise. Add more. Keep going. Eventually you reach pure noise — a standard Gaussian. Crucially, this only truly reaches Gaussian at t → ∞. Then learn to reverse each noising step.

Forward noising process — "destroying" information over time

THE CORE PROBLEM

Because diffusion first spreads points outward (adding noise) and then pulls them inward (denoising), the reverse trajectories are curved and inefficient.

An ODE solver has to follow these curves with many small steps — expensive at inference time.

Why diffusion paths curve
Flow Matching — Why Fix A Process At All?
The flow matching insight LEARN THE MORPH DIRECTLY

The paper asks: why do we even define a fixed noising process? All we ultimately want is to take a sample from P₀ (Gaussian noise) and transform it into a sample from P₁ (data). Can't we just learn how to smoothly morph one distribution into the other, without prescribing the path?

1D intuition — morphing a distribution over time t ∈ [0, 1]
✅ Flow matching actually reaches P₁ at finite time t = 1. Unlike diffusion where you need t → ∞ to guarantee you've reached Gaussian noise, here the math is clean: start at t=0, finish at t=1, done.
